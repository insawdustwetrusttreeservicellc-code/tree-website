Florida Storm Prep Checklist

Before hurricane season, homeowners should inspect trees for dead limbs, cracked branches, leaning trunks, limbs touching roofs, and trees growing too close to structures.

Signs a Tree May Be Dangerous

Watch for hollow trunks, large dead limbs, fresh cracks, mushrooms around the base, sudden leaning, bark separation, and limbs hanging over bedrooms, driveways, or power areas.

After-Storm Tree Safety

Do not stand under cracked limbs or try to cut storm-loaded branches without experience. Tensioned limbs can snap, roll, or kick back unexpectedly.

Need a Local Tree Safety Check?

If you are worried about a tree before storm season, send photos or call for a free estimate. We can tell you if it needs trimming, removal, cleanup, or closer inspection.
